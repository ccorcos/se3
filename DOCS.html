3D Rotation Matrices

\(R_x\) is known as roll, \(R_y\) is pich, and \(R_z\) is yaw.

\[R_x(\theta) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \cos \theta & -\sin \theta \\[3pt] 0 & \sin \theta & \cos \theta \\[3pt] \end{bmatrix} \\[6pt] R_y(\theta) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & 0 & \sin \theta \\[3pt] 0 & 1 & 0 \\[3pt] -\sin \theta & 0 & \cos \theta \\ \end{bmatrix} \\[6pt] R_z(\theta) = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & -\sin \theta & 0 \\[3pt] \sin \theta & \cos \theta & 0\\[3pt] 0 & 0 & 1\\ \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt]\]

Given yaw, pitch, and roll angles, \(\alpha\), \(\beta\), and \(\gamma\), we can combine them to get the roll-pitch-yaw rotation matrix:

\[R = R_z(\alpha) \, R_y(\beta) \, R_x(\gamma)\]

3D Homogenous Transforms

Homogenous transforms combine rotation \(R\) with position \(t\) like so:

\[t = \begin{bmatrix} x\\ y\\ z \end{bmatrix} \\[6pt]\]

\[T = \begin{bmatrix} R & t\\ 0_{1\times3} & 1 \\[3pt] \end{bmatrix} \\[6pt]\]

Thus, when combining transformations with multiplication, we get a translation from the previous orientation followed by a rotation:

\[T_1 \, T_2 = \begin{bmatrix} R_1\,R_2 & t_1 + R_1\,t_2\\ 0_{1\times3} & 1 \\[3pt] \end{bmatrix} \\[6pt]\]
